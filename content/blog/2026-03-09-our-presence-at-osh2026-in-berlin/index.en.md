This year in Mai (23-24.05.2026), the TU Berlin will transform into an international hub
for the open hardware community.
The Open Hardware Summit (OHS2026), organized by the Open Source Hardware Association 
(OSHWA), will take place in Germany for the first time in 2026.
The Open.Make project 
(and our partner project LAUDS Factories) will be presented quite extensively.
We are indeed involved in the organisation as the local partner, proposing a specific
course for TU Berlin students (this allowed to grow our teaching port-folio 
and provide the 
TU Berlin rooms for free).

Around 300 participants from all over the world are expected to attend,
including developers, artists, start-ups, researchers, and makers 
who are enthusiastic about open technologies.
As in the past, the two-day program will feature lectures, 
panels, hands-on workshops, and exhibitions featuring open hardware and 
diversity in the maker scence, with the topics of feminism and decolonisation at the front. 
Topics such as sustainable hardware development, 
repairability, open-source chips, and community-driven manufacturing and production will be the focus. 
In addition, there will be networking formats and local satellite events 
in Berlin's maker and hacker spaces.

For instance, we will run a satellite workshop on Friday 22nd to develop
business model framework for LAUDS Factories. While we are still discussing whether the mobile lab will be exhibited
during the event, we will be present with one talk and one workshop.

We are looking to see you there !
